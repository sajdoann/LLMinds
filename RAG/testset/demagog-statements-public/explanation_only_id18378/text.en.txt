<p>On August 27, 2019, President Zeman <a href="https://www.vlada.cz/cz/media-centrum/aktualne/predseda-vlady-uvedl-do-funkce-noveho-ministra-kultury-lubomira-zaoralka-175732/">appointed</a> Lubomír Zaorálek (ČSSD) as the new Minister of Culture. After taking the oath, Miloš Zeman <a href="https://www.facebook.com/CT24.cz/videos/386820792025695/">said</a> , among other things:</p><p> &quot; <em>What is the broad path that leads every minister of culture into oblivion? The fact that he goes to various receptions, openings, premieres, festivals, and so on, and so on. Sometimes he awards a grant or subsidy, and of course the media sphere applauds him. Cultural monuments, Mr. Minister, do not applaud. Cultural monuments are silent and decay. Yet these monuments are the minutiae of the Czech landscape. And we have been unable to supplement these minutiae, which were created by our ancestors, with almost nothing else. It is our shame, but let us at least try to save what others have built.</em> &quot;</p><p> The National Heritage Institute <a href="https://www.npu.cz/pamatkovy-fond">states</a> that there are over 700 dilapidated cultural monuments in the Czech Republic out of a total of 40,000. These include, for example, <a href="https://www.npu.cz/cs/npu-a-pamatkova-pece/pamatky-a-pamatkova-pece/pamatkovy-fond/seznam-ohrozenych-nemovitych-pamatek/32147-oranzerie-hrad-veveri-brno">the orangery</a> at Veveří Castle in Brno, <a href="https://www.npu.cz/cs/npu-a-pamatkova-pece/pamatky-a-pamatkova-pece/pamatkovy-fond/seznam-ohrozenych-nemovitych-pamatek/42181-zimni-zahrada-zamek-velke-opatovice">the winter garden</a> at the Velké Opatovice Chateau or <a href="https://www.npu.cz/cs/npu-a-pamatkova-pece/pamatky-a-pamatkova-pece/pamatkovy-fond/seznam-ohrozenych-nemovitych-pamatek/46568-socha-sv-floriana-areal-frantiskanskeho-klastera-brno"></a> <a href="https://www.npu.cz/cs/npu-a-pamatkova-pece/pamatky-a-pamatkova-pece/pamatkovy-fond/seznam-ohrozenych-nemovitych-pamatek/38714-zamek-v-hornim-marsove">castle</a> in Horní Maršov.</p><p> Lubomír Zaorálek was appointed to the position after a three-and-a-half-month-long dispute over the head of the Ministry of Culture department. Former Minister of Culture Antonín Staněk (ČSSD) <a href="https://ct24.ceskatelevize.cz/domaci/2815571-cesko-potrebuje-noveho-ministra-kultury-kritizovany-stanek-ohlasil-rezignaci"></a><a href="https://ct24.ceskatelevize.cz/domaci/2815571-cesko-potrebuje-noveho-ministra-kultury-kritizovany-stanek-ohlasil-rezignaci"></a> <a href="https://ct24.ceskatelevize.cz/domaci/2815571-cesko-potrebuje-noveho-ministra-kultury-kritizovany-stanek-ohlasil-rezignaci">submitted</a> his resignation in mid-May, at the request of the head of the ČSSD, Jan Hamáček. President Zeman <a href="https://www.irozhlas.cz/zpravy-domov/antonin-stanek-milos-zeman-demise_1905281745_dok">did not accept</a> Staňek&#39;s resignation. The head of the ČSSD, Jan Hamáček <a href="https://ct24.ceskatelevize.cz/domaci/2827265-prezident-neprijal-demisi-ministra-kultury-stanka">, said</a> that he would therefore ask Prime Minister Babiš to propose to the president the dismissal of Antonín Staňek from office. In such a case, President Zeman would have to dismiss Antonín Staňek according to <a href="https://www.zakonyprolidi.cz/cs/1993-1#cl74">Article 74 of Constitutional Act No. 1/1993 Coll. of the Constitution</a> of the Czech Republic. At the same time, <a href="https://ct24.ceskatelevize.cz/domaci/2827265-prezident-neprijal-demisi-ministra-kultury-stanka">he proposed</a> the deputy chairman of the ČSSD, Michal Šmarda, as the new Minister of Culture. Andrej Babiš proposed the dismissal of the Minister of Culture to the president, but he <a href="https://ct24.ceskatelevize.cz/domaci/2883683-zive-stanek-se-louci-s-ministerstvem-kultury-podekovat-mu-prisel-premier-babis">remained</a> in office until the end of July. Then, until the appointment of Lubomír Zaorálek on August 27, Deputy René Schreier temporarily <a href="https://ct24.ceskatelevize.cz/domaci/2883683-zive-stanek-se-louci-s-ministerstvem-kultury-podekovat-mu-prisel-premier-babis">took over</a> the ministry. President Zeman <a href="https://www.hrad.cz/cs/pro-media/tiskove-zpravy/aktualni-tiskove-zpravy/prohlaseni-prezidenta-republiky-1-14950#from-list">refused</a> to appoint the potential candidate Michal Šmarda in August, according to him, he was not professionally competent. Michal Šmarda finally <a href="https://ct24.ceskatelevize.cz/domaci/2901029-smarda-pro-ct-ve-vlade-andreje-babise-byt-nechci">withdrew</a> his nomination for the post of Minister of Culture a few days after the president announced his nomination.</p><p> The ČSSD therefore <a href="https://ct24.ceskatelevize.cz/domaci/2902175-novym-ministrem-kultury-ma-byt-zaoralek-potvrdil-hamacek-zeman-souhlasi">proposed</a> Lubomír Zaorálek, who <a href="https://ct24.ceskatelevize.cz/domaci/2902175-novym-ministrem-kultury-ma-byt-zaoralek-potvrdil-hamacek-zeman-souhlasi">has been</a> a member of the ČSSD since 1994. Since then, he has held a number of positions, and <a href="https://ct24.ceskatelevize.cz/domaci/2902826-zaoralek-se-v-politice-pohybuje-od-80-let-se-zemanem-mel-spory">is</a> now the chairman of the parliamentary foreign affairs committee, a member of the committee for science, education, culture, youth and sports, and a foreign policy expert for the ČSSD. The new minister of culture&#39;s <a href="https://ct24.ceskatelevize.cz/domaci/2907302-zive-prezident-jmenuje-lubomira-zaoralka-ministrem-kultury">priorities</a> include, among other things, increasing salaries in culture and overall stabilization of the department.</p>
