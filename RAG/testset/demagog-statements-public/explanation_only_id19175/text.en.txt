<p><a href="https://www.zakonyprolidi.cz/cs/2000-240">The Crisis Act</a> (Act No. 240/200 Coll.), which MP Bartoš mentions, envisages the establishment of a Central Crisis Staff in cases of a crisis situation, which is understood to mean:</p><p> &quot; <i>An emergency under the Integrated Rescue System Act, a disruption of critical infrastructure or other danger in which a state of danger, emergency or state of threat to the state is declared (hereinafter referred to as</i> &#39; <i>crisis state</i> &#39; <i>)</i> &quot;<i> </i>( <a href="https://www.zakonyprolidi.cz/cs/2000-240#p2-1-b">§ 2, letter b)</a> .</p><p> According to <a href="https://www.zakonyprolidi.cz/cs/2000-240#p4-1-c">Section 4, Paragraph 1, Letter c),</a> the Government &quot; <i>establishes the Central Crisis Staff as its working body to resolve crisis situations</i> &quot; <i>.</i></p><p> The Central Crisis Staff pursuant to Article 2(4) as amended by the Government <a href="https://www.vlada.cz/assets/ppov/brs/pracovni-vybory/ustredni-krizovy-stab/statut-2020.pdf">Resolution</a> (.pdf, page 1) of March 15, 2020, in particular:</p><p> <i>a) ensures operational cooperation with crisis management bodies of international organizations,</i></p><p> <i>b) ensures the assessment of the development of the situation, the content and adequacy of measures taken by administrative authorities and bodies of territorial self-governing units and informs the State Security Council,</i></p><p> <i>c) assesses, discusses and coordinates the adoption of interdepartmental measures proposed by ministries,</i></p><p> <i><strong>d) prepares proposals for measures to resolve the situation for the State Security Council or the government and documents for making decisions requiring approval by the government or, where appropriate, the Parliament of the Czech Republic.</strong></i></p><p> In addition to the chairman of the staff, other members of the staff are the deputies of individual ministers, the chairman of the State Material Reserves Administration, the Police President of the Czech Republic, the Director General of the Fire Rescue Service of the Czech Republic, the Chief of the General Staff of the Army of the Czech Republic, the Director of the National Office for Cyber and Information Security, the Chief Hygienist, the Director of the State Institute of Health and the Head of the Office of the Government of the Czech Republic (. <a href="https://www.vlada.cz/assets/ppov/brs/pracovni-vybory/ustredni-krizovy-stab/statut-2020.pdf">pdf</a> , p. 2). According to Article 7 <i>, “The staff may invite representatives of other ministries, other administrative authorities, bodies of territorial self-governing units and other experts to its meetings.”</i> (. <a href="https://www.vlada.cz/assets/ppov/brs/pracovni-vybory/ustredni-krizovy-stab/statut-2020.pdf">pdf</a> , p. 4)</p><p> In connection with the spread of the coronavirus infection, a state of emergency was <a href="https://www.vlada.cz/cz/media-centrum/aktualne/vyhlaseni-nouzoveho-stavu-180234/">declared</a> in the Czech Republic in accordance with <a href="https://www.zakonyprolidi.cz/cs/1998-110">the Constitutional Act on the Security of the Czech Republic</a> (Constitutional Act No. 110/1998 Coll.) from 2:00 p.m. on March 12, 2020 for a period of 30 days.</p><p> Since the declaration of the state of emergency, only the government has discussed and decided on the measures. On the day of its declaration, Prime Minister Babiš <a href="https://www.vlada.cz/cz/media-centrum/tiskove-konference/tiskova-konference-po-mimoradnem-jednani-vlady--12--brezna-2020-180249/">expressed himself</a> on the method of crisis management during the state of emergency as follows: <i>&quot;We have clearly agreed that this state of emergency is managed by the government. I would be very happy if speculation about how it should be managed and whether it should be managed stopped. We have clearly imposed on the government members to ask the government for prior approval for measures related to the declaration of the state of emergency that a member of the government or the ministry managed by him takes within the scope of his powers, even before they are implemented.&quot;</i></p><p> Until the establishment of the Central Crisis Staff (i.e. between March 12 and 15, 2020), the government decided, for example, on:</p><ul><li> <a high-risk countries (.pdf),</li><li> <a On March 30, 2020, he was <a href="https://ct24.ceskatelevize.cz/domaci/3069180-ustredni-krizovy-stab-mel-prevzit-hamacek-prymula-se-chce-soustredit-na-organizaci">replaced</a> as Chairman of the Central Crisis Staff by Minister of the Interior Jan Hamáček. Deputy Prymula was <a href="https://www.frekvence1.cz/clanky/zpravy/prymula-stanul-v-cele-centralniho-ridiciho-tymu-covid-19.shtml">appointed</a> Chairman of the COVID-19 Central Steering Team, a newly <a href="https://www.vlada.cz/assets/media-centrum/aktualne/01-Statut-CRT_final.pdf">established</a> advisory body to the government that coordinates the organization of the so-called smart quarantine.</p>
