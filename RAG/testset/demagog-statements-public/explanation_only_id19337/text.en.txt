<p>The meeting of the House Committee on Security described in the statement, at which the &quot;information of the Director of the Security Information Service (BIS) on some articles published in the previous two weeks in the online edition of the Respekt periodical&quot; was <a href="https://www.psp.cz/sqw/text/orig2.sqw?idd=173469">discussed</a> ( <i>.doc)</i> , took place on May 6, 2020 and was <a href="https://www.psp.cz/sqw/hp.sqw?k=4916&amp;dx=20200506">not public</a> .</p><p> The meeting took place in response to <a href="https://www.respekt.cz/tydenik/2020/18/muz-s-ricinem">an article</a> in the weekly magazine Respekt, in which Ondřej Kundra described the arrival of a man from Russia with a diplomatic passport who had ricin (a poison) in his briefcase. He also stated that security forces were aware of the traveler&#39;s arrival and assessed the man as an immediate risk to a pair of Czech politicians whose actions in recent months had drawn the ire of Moscow. These politicians were said to be Ondřej Kolář and Zdeněk Hřib. Ondřej Kundra supported this information with unspecified Respekt sources.</p><p> According to sources from the Czech Press Office, the Security Committee <a href="https://www.ceskenoviny.cz/zpravy/reakce-bezpecnostnich-slozek-v-kauze-ricin-byla-podle-vyboru-adekvatni/1887589">found</a> the response of the Czech security forces to be adequate.
