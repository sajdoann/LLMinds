<p>Václav Havel <a href="https://ct24.ceskatelevize.cz/domaci/1061767-zeman-bude-udelovat-jen-nektere-milosti-tridit-je-bude-ministerstvo">pardoned</a> a total of 860 people between 1993 and 2002, and more than 1,000 more while he was still federal president. In contrast, Václav Klaus <a href="https://ct24.ceskatelevize.cz/domaci/1061767-zeman-bude-udelovat-jen-nektere-milosti-tridit-je-bude-ministerstvo">pardoned</a> 412 people in his 10 years in office. Miloš Zeman did indeed <a href="http://demagog.cz/vyrok/21795">grant</a> 21 pardons.</p><p> Let us now focus on the individual cases of pardons granted by Václav Havel, which Miloš Zeman criticizes. Martin Odložil, the son of the famous gymnast Věra Čáslavská, <a href="https://zpravy.aktualne.cz/domaci/kajinek-neni-prvni-havel-a-klaus-take-davali-sporne-milosti/r~888b87d8372511e79680002590604f2e/v~sl:9e8fcf6d9cb6f33bc09339aea463d111/">hit</a> his father Josef Odložil during an argument in 1993. He hit his head in the unfortunate fall and died. The police then charged Martin Odložil with grievous bodily harm resulting in death and he was sent to prison for four years without a final decision by the court. Before the appeals court could decide on the case, President Havel granted Odložil a pardon. Although Martin Odložil was not charged with murder, in 2011 <a href="https://www.lidovky.cz/relax/lide/pohnute-osudy-syn-very-caslavske-zabil-sveho-otce-od-vezeni-ho-zachranila-milost.A160303_201015_lide_ele">he spoke</a> of killing his father. The term “patricide” is nevertheless very simplistic.</p><p> In 1992, Marta Chadimová <a href="https://zpravy.aktualne.cz/domaci/kajinek-neni-prvni-havel-a-klaus-take-davali-sporne-milosti/r~888b87d8372511e79680002590604f2e/v~sl:0eb05e51241930648939959bb7240a9f/">was</a> <a href="https://www.idnes.cz/zpravy/domaci/chadimova-po-letech-vyuzila-milost.A031031_123202_domaci_jan">charged</a> with fraud, forgery and alteration of public documents and obstruction of execution of an official decision, in connection with the restitution of a house on Loretánské náměstí. The then President Havel granted her a pardon in 1995. <a href="https://www.ceskenoviny.cz/zpravy/marta-chadimova-prijala-prezidentskou-milost-jeji-proces-konci/32730">He said</a> on Czech Television: <i>&quot;She is in custody for the third time, (...) the trial has been going on for three and a half years, there is no end in sight, the verdict is not in sight and she is very seriously ill.&quot;</i> Chadimová did not accept the pardon until 2003, when the trial had already dragged on for over 10 years. Pavel Rychetský was <a href="https://www.idnes.cz/zpravy/domaci/soudce-jiri-kulvejt-zkolaboval-v-budove-vrchniho-soudu-v-praze.A130129_144200_domaci_jav">indeed</a> one of Marta Chadimová&#39;s defense attorneys. Miloš Zeman calls Marta Chadimová a restitution fraudster. However, a final verdict was never handed down, so this description is again relatively simplistic. We therefore evaluate the statement as true with reservations.</p><p> Finally, let us note that Miloš Zeman, in <a href="https://www.hrad.cz/cs/prezident-cr/soucasny-prezident-cr/vybrane-projevy-a-rozhovory/rozhovor-prezidenta-republiky-pro-porad-ftv-prima-partie-terezie-tomankove-16390">an interview</a> for CNN Prima NEWS, compares these controversial pardons by Václav Havel with the pardon <a href="https://www.ceskenoviny.cz/zpravy/prezident-zeman-udelil-milost-obvinenemu-sefovi-lanske-obory-balakovi/2184952">he granted</a> to his <a href="https://www.hrad.cz/cs/prezident-cr/kancelar-prezidenta-republiky/organizacni-struktura">subordinate</a> Miloš Balák, head of the Lány <a href="https://ct24.ceskatelevize.cz/domaci/3459550-balak-z-lesni-spravy-lany-musi-na-tri-roky-do-vezeni-potvrdil-soud">Forest Administration</a> . He was <a href="https://www.ceskenoviny.cz/zpravy/prezident-zeman-udelil-milost-obvinenemu-sefovi-lanske-obory-balakovi/2184952">finally</a> convicted of influencing a public contract in the Lánská obor. President <a href="https://www.hrad.cz/cs/pro-media/tiskove-zpravy/aktualni-tiskove-zpravy/prezident-republiky-udelil-milost-16350#from-list">Zeman</a> justified his decision to grant the pardon, among other things, by saying that, according to him, Balák is <i>an “exceptionally hardworking and qualified senior employee”</i> .</p>
